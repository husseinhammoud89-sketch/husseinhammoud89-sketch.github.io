Context

Designing for a broken financial system.

Lebanon's banking crisis left millions of people without reliable access to their own money. Traditional banking apps were irrelevant for a large portion of the population. WeePay was built to fill that gap: a trusted, accessible digital wallet that didn't require a bank account to get started.

I joined as the product designer responsible for the full mobile experience from zero to a production-ready, responsive app serving real users across Lebanon.

"We need people to trust us with their money before they trust any bank. That means every screen has to feel safe."

Research and Validation

How the decisions were grounded.

Before designing, I ran a competitive analysis of regional e-wallets and remittance services to understand what users expected and where existing apps created friction, which directly shaped decisions like upfront fee transparency and single-screen transfers. Flows were reviewed and signed off with the WeePay product and business team to align on scope and feasibility.

Design Approach

Trust and simplicity above everything.

For a financial product in a high-distrust environment, the primary design challenge wasn't just usability it was credibility. Every interaction needed to feel safe, intentional, and transparent.

Core flows designed

I designed the full suite of WeePay's core product flows: onboarding (zero-fee sign-up with minimal friction), money transfers (domestic and international via MoneyGram), bill payment for mobile and utility providers, QR code-based shopping at licensed merchants, and cash top-up and withdrawal through the agent network. Each flow was designed mobile-first, then adapted for web.

Cash in a digital product

Most users entered and exited the system through physical cash agents, not card top-ups. The agent locator and the deposit and withdrawal flows were treated as first-class product surfaces, not an afterthought. For this audience, the bridge between cash and digital was the product's most used feature.

Key Decisions

Designing for distrust.

Every fee visible before every confirmation

In a market where banks had frozen people's savings, hidden costs were the fastest way to lose a user forever. The standard fintech pattern of revealing fees at the final step was off the table. Every transfer flow shows the full cost breakdown upfront, before the user commits to anything. Slower funnel, stronger trust, and trust was the product.

Plain language over banking language

Early drafts used conventional financial copy: beneficiary, remittance, transaction settlement. For an audience that actively distrusted banks, this vocabulary worked against us. Every flow was rewritten in plain conversational language. You are not initiating a remittance, you are sending money to your sister.

Onboarding with nothing to lose

Sign-up requires no bank account, no deposit, and no commitment. The decision was to let users explore the full app before putting a single lira in. People in a financial crisis do not trust first and verify later, they verify first. Removing every barrier to that verification was the single most important onboarding decision.

Impact

What the design made possible.

  • I designed flows that let people store, spend, and transfer money in both LBP and Fresh USD without a bank account, critical access during Lebanon's banking collapse.
  • I designed the single-screen peer-to-peer transfer and bill-payment flows, removing the multi-step friction that defined traditional banking apps.
  • I designed the cross-border remittance flow with CashUnited, letting users receive money from abroad and cash out through a nationwide agent network, a primary adoption driver.
  • I built trust into the experience through zero hidden fees, upfront fee transparency, biometric security, and real-time spend tracking.
Outcomes

A digital gateway where none existed.

WeePay launched as an accessible, trustworthy alternative to traditional banking for users in Lebanon. It shipped on iOS and Android in both USD and LBP, bridging cash and digital through a nationwide agent network.
