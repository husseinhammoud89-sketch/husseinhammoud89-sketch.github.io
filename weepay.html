WeePay — financial access without a bank account

Designed a mobile-first payment app for Lebanon's challenging monetary landscape. Send and receive money locally and internationally, pay bills, and shop via QR code. No bank account required.

Client
Klevr / WeePay
Role
Senior Product Designer
Timeline
Jan 2022 to Aug 2023
Platform
iOS · Android · Web
Context

Designing for a broken financial system.

Lebanon's banking crisis left millions of people without reliable access to their own money. Traditional banking apps were irrelevant for a large portion of the population. WeePay was built to fill that gap: a trusted, accessible digital wallet that didn't require a bank account to get started.

I joined as the product designer responsible for the full mobile experience — from zero to a production-ready, responsive app used by real clinic clients and individual users.

Design Approach

Trust and simplicity above everything.

For a financial product in a high-distrust environment, the primary design challenge wasn't just usability — it was credibility. Every interaction needed to feel safe, intentional, and transparent.

Core flows designed

I designed the full suite of WeePay's core product flows: onboarding (zero-fee sign-up with minimal friction), money transfers (domestic and international), bill payment, and QR code-based shopping. Each flow was designed mobile-first, then adapted for web.

Clinic payment module

WeePay also powered clinic payment flows for multiple healthcare clients. I designed and shipped responsive web experiences for these clients, improving patient-facing billing flows and reducing payment drop-off. Cross-platform visual consistency was maintained across the mobile app, web dashboard, and client-facing materials.

Outcomes

A digital gateway where none existed.

WeePay launched as an accessible, trustworthy alternative to traditional banking for users in Lebanon. The product served both individual users and clinic clients, with consistent design quality across all touchpoints.
