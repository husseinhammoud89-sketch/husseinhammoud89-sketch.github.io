Overview

What if candidates were the product, not the applicants?

Traditional job boards put all the burden on candidates. You apply, wait, get rejected, and never know why. JobRivals flipped this completely: candidates create profiles, and employers browse and compete to hire them — like products on a shelf.

I was brought in as the sole designer to take this concept from zero to a fully shipped product experience — candidate app, recruiter dashboard, and everything in between.

"We want candidates to feel like action figures in a store. Employers pick them, not the other way around."

My Role

Solo designer, full product scope.

Over 11 months I owned the entire design process: user research, information architecture, interaction design, visual design, and developer handoff. No design team, no design system inherited from a previous iteration. Everything built from scratch.

Key design decisions

The biggest challenge was the pre-signup assessment flow. To maintain quality on both sides, I invented a screening experience that surfaced candidate quality before account creation. Candidates answer a short set of questions upfront — this data feeds the matching algorithm and gives employers meaningful signal before they even view a full profile.

This became one of the product's core differentiators. Employers weren't browsing generic CVs — they were browsing pre-qualified candidates with visible compatibility scores.

Responsive across web and mobile

The candidate experience was primarily mobile. The recruiter experience was primarily desktop. I designed and maintained both simultaneously, ensuring visual consistency across very different interaction contexts and screen densities.
